The Christian Claim

قُلْ إِن كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَٰنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ

Qul in kāna lil-Raḥmāni waladun fa-anā awwalu al-ʿābidīn

“Say: If the All-Merciful had a child, then I would be the first to worship” (Qur’an 43:81)

The word “worship” (ʿābidīn) means accepting the child as divine, supporting the idea of Jesus as God’s son.

Premise 3: Fits Christian Belief

This matches the Christian view that Jesus is God’s son, part of the Trinity.

Conclusion

Qur’an 43:81 supports the possibility of God having a son, aligning with Christian theology.

This analysis shows the Christian claim misreads the verse. Qur’an 43:81 uses a special Arabic style called al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ (impossible condition) to reject the idea of God having a child, targeting Meccan pagans and Christians. Language Analysis: Meaning of al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ and ʿābidīn

Introduction: Words Matter

To understand Qur’an 43:81, we need to know what its words mean in Arabic, especially al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ (impossible condition) and ʿābidīn (worshippers or rejectors).

Arabic Term Transliteration Meaning Example Usage
الشرط الممتنع al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ Impossible condition, denying the premise “If the sun rose at night, I’d be amazed”
عابدين ʿābidīn Worshippers or rejectors “I disdain to mock him” (al-Farazdaq)
Example: Word Meanings in Arabic

In Arabic, ʿābidīn can mean “worshippers” or “rejectors,” depending on context. Al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ is a style that says something’s impossible to shut down a bad idea.

وَأَعْبَدُ أَنْ أَهْجُو كُلَيْبًا بِدَارِمِ

Wa-aʿbudu an ahjū Kulayban bi-Dārim

“I disdain to satirize Kulayb with Dārim” (al-Farazdaq, per Lisān al-ʿArab)

قَالَ إِنِّي لَا يَقْتُلُنِي اللَّهُ إِنْ شَاءَ

Qāla innī lā yaqtulunī Allāhu in shā’a

“He said: Surely Allah will not kill me if He wills” (Qur’an 2:258)

Application to Qur’an 43:81

Harold W. Attridge notes that biblical rhetoric uses conditionals to reject false ideas, like in Qur’an 43:81. The word ʿābidīn means “rejectors” here, as old Arabic books like Lisān al-ʿArab (Ibn Manẓūr, d. 711 AH) say it can mean “to disdain.” The phrase “if… then” (in kāna) is al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘, a style that says the idea of God having a child is impossible, per al-Zamakhsharī (al-Kashshāf, d. 538 AH). A Shi‘i teaching says Prophet Muhammad was the first creation (nūr Muḥammadī, al-Kāfī, Vol. 1, p. 442), so he’d know God has no child and reject it. Christians misread “worship” as accepting a son, but the verse shuts down Meccan pagan (Qur’an 43:16–19) and Christian (Qur’an 9:30) claims.

Conclusion

The words ʿābidīn and al-sharṭ al-mumteni‘ show Qur’an 43:81 rejects God having a child, not supports it, making the Christian view wrong.
